IEEE Standards

What are IEEE Standards?
IEEE Standards are globally recognized guidelines and specifications developed by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) to ensure quality, safety, interoperability, and innovation in technology and engineering fields.
Why are IEEE Standards Important?
They provide a common framework for engineers, researchers, and organizations, enabling compatibility and reliability across products and services worldwide.
Examples of Popular IEEE Standards
  • IEEE 802.11: Wireless LAN (Wi-Fi) standards
  • IEEE 802.3: Ethernet networking standards
  • IEEE 754: Floating-point arithmetic standard
  • IEEE 488: GPIB (General Purpose Interface Bus) standard
How to Access IEEE Standards?
Students and professionals can access IEEE standards through the official IEEE Standards website or via institutional subscriptions.
